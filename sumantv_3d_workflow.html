Production Pipeline
📝
STAGE 01 Script & VO Blueprint Day 1 · 2–3 hrs

Write the Telugu VO script with scene-by-scene breakdown. Define what each visual must communicate — especially the core concept scene. Timing is everything: each line of VO must map to a specific visual beat.

Claude / ChatGPT
Google Docs
SumanTV tip: Write VO in Telugu first, then write English visual descriptions per scene. Keep VO lines short — 6–10 words max per line for natural breathing rhythm.
🎙️
STAGE 02 Voice Over Recording Day 1 · 1–2 hrs

Record the Telugu VO with proper pacing. Keep silence gaps between sections — these are where visual cuts happen. Export as WAV at 48kHz. This audio file becomes the master timeline for all visual generation.

ElevenLabs (AI VO)
Human VO (preferred)
Audacity (cleanup)
ElevenLabs note: Telugu voice quality is improving. Use "Multilingual v2" model. Still best to use a human VO for authenticity on health content — trust factor matters.
🎨
STAGE 03 Static Frame Generation Day 1–2 · 3–4 hrs

Generate all key still frames — cutaway diagrams, opening room shots, infographic bases, closing frames. These become either standalone scenes or the starting image for video generation in Stage 4.

Midjourney v6
Adobe Firefly
DALL·E 3
Workflow: Generate 4 variants per scene → pick best → upscale (U1–U4) → export PNG. Save all raw exports in a /frames folder organized by scene number.
⚡
STAGE 04 3D Motion & Video Generation Day 2–3 · 4–6 hrs

Animate the static frames into motion clips. This is the highest-effort stage. Use image-to-video for consistent look, text-to-video for abstract particle scenes. Generate 2x more clips than needed — only ~60% will be usable.

Kling AI · Motion loops
RunwayML Gen-3 · Hero scenes
Sora · Physics scenes
Hailuo · Camera moves
Scene routing guide:
Particle physics (compressor, airflow) → Kling AI
Split comparisons, narrative transitions → RunwayML
Camera pullbacks, environment shots → Hailuo
Physically accurate fluid/gas motion → Sora
🧱
STAGE 05 3D Model Assets (Optional) Day 2 · 2 hrs

For episodes requiring detailed component walkthroughs (AC internals, human organs, machinery), generate 3D models and render them as turntable videos. Skip this stage for simpler explainer topics.

Meshy AI · Text→3D
Spline · Interactive 3D
Blender · Rendering
Meshy tip: Use "Turntable render" export — gives you a 360° rotating video clip ready to drop into the timeline. No Blender skills needed.
✂️
STAGE 06 Edit, Text Overlays & Sound Day 3–4 · 4–5 hrs

Assemble all clips on timeline synced to VO audio. Add Telugu text overlays, animated labels, sound design (whoosh, hum, bass), and color grade. This stage turns raw AI clips into a finished broadcast-quality video.

CapCut Pro · Primary
DaVinci Resolve · Grade
Envato Elements · SFX
Non-negotiable edits: Cut every 3–4 sec · Slow zoom on key frames · Motion blur on particles · Bass drop on compressor scene · Telugu labels via CapCut text (never rely on AI to render Telugu)
🚀
STAGE 07 Thumbnail & Publish Day 4 · 1–2 hrs

Create a high-contrast before/after thumbnail using the hook frame (hot room) and closing frame (cool room). Write keyword-rich Telugu title and description. Schedule across relevant channels in your health network.

Canva Pro · Thumbnail
VidIQ · SEO
YouTube Studio
Thumbnail formula: Before (red/hot) LEFT + After (blue/cool) RIGHT + Telugu text + face of curiosity or shock. This pattern consistently outperforms pure 3D thumbnails on Telugu health channels.
