Chapter 01 — The Disconnection

Life back home requires a dozen different tools.
Families deserve one.

For the modern NRI, staying connected to India is a daily logistics problem disguised as an emotional one. Eight thousand miles, thirteen time zones, and no single trusted hand on the ground.

"Every month I'm calling four different relatives, three apps, and praying someone answers. I just want to know my parents are okay." — Composite from NRI interviews, 2026
01

No Indian phone or bank

Locked out of the UPI revolution. Forced to carry cash, wire money through relatives, or delay routine payments for weeks.

02

Medical emergencies, 8,000 miles away

A late-night call about a parent's fall, a hospital admission, a prescription that needs filling. No verified local contact to respond.

03

Logistical nightmares

Blind spots in international shipping, unreliable local couriers, property documents that require physical presence, and receipts lost in translation.

04

Fragmented care & coordination

Utility bills, health insurance, elder care, weddings, pujas, property management—nine apps and zero continuity between them.

05

The trust deficit

Without a verified name behind the service, every introduction is a risk. Without a face on the ground, every transaction is a leap of faith.
